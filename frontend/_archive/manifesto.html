The Code of Harmony

Why a Violinist is the Best Person to Fix Your Data Pipeline.

We tend to think of "Systems" and "Art" as opposing forces. One is rigid, the other fluid. One is code, the other emotion.

I disagree.

For 20 years, I operated within one of the most complex, high-stakes systems in human history: The Orchestra.

An orchestra is a distributed system. It has latency (the speed of sound). It has concurrency (100 players). It has a single source of truth (the score). And when it fails, it fails publicly.

The Pivot

Two years ago, I began translating these principles into code. I didn't just learn "Javascript"; I learned how to orchestrate information.

Projectorium AI isn't just a music app. It's a study in asynchronous consensus—how do we get a group of people to agree on a creative direction without being in the same room?

Space Time Tuning Machine isn't just performance art. It's a temporal database—a way to visualize time not as a straight line, but as a loop, a cycle, a wave.

The Future

I am now applying this "Polyphonic Systems Design" to civic infrastructure. Our public institutions are bad orchestras. They are playing out of tune. They are ignoring the conductor.

I am here to fix the score.
